Introduction About Turtles

The term "turtle" is derived from the French words "turtle" and "tortoise." Since it is a common name, it can be used regardless of taxonomic classification. It could be used to refer to the entire order throughout North America. In Britain, the word is only applied to sea turtles, not to freshwater terrapins or terrestrial tortoises. Non-marine turtles were historically referred to as tortoises in Australia, which lacks actual tortoises (family Testudinidae), but more lately the term "turtle" has been adopted to refer to the entire group. There are 360 species of turtles, including freshwater terrapins and land-dwelling tortoises, both currently extant and recently extinct.

What do turtles eat

Although they are omnivores as hatchlings, adults are referred regarded as herbivores. Their main food sources are algae, seaweed, and seagrasses. Greens can rip grasses and seaweed and can scrape algae off of rocks with the help of their finely serrated (sawlike) beak.

How big can turtles grow

The olive and Kemp's ridley sea turtles are the smallest species, with shells that only reach a length of 70 cm (just over 2 ft) and a weight of up to 100 lbs (45 kg). The largest marine turtles are leatherbacks. Leatherbacks often weigh 300 to 500 kg (660 to 1,100 lbs) and are 1.5 to 2 m (4-6 ft) long..

Turtle Behaviour

In the ocean, flatback turtles may float at the surface for hours while appearing to sleep or enjoy the sun. Seabirds frequently perch on the flatbacks' backs.

Saving Sea Turtles

Few experiences compare to viewing a sea turtle up close, as divers and snorkelers around the world are aware. The giant reptiles glide through the water with otherworldly ease; the largest can reach lengths of six feet and weights of three quarters of a ton.

Habitats of sea Turtles

Most species' adults can be found in bays, estuaries, lagoons, and shallow coastal waters. Some even go out on the open ocean. Some species' juveniles can be found both at sea and in bays and estuaries.
