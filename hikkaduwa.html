About Hikkaduwa Hatchery

About 2 kilometers north of Hikkaduwa, on the beach, is the Turtle Hatchery. The hatchery is a tiny, private research facility that demonstrates the turtles' progressive growth and works to preserve marine turtles, which are in risk of extinction. For an additional price, there are some locations where you can also get the chance to release newborn turtles into the huge ocean. March through April are the greatest months to go and watch the turtles.

Some of the best scuba diving in Sri Lanka can be found in Hikkaduwa at special dive locations including wrecks and breathtaking reefs. It is also said to boast Sri Lanka's second-best surfing.Restaurants, shacks, and taverns with a view of the water along the shore. The magnificent tourist attractions in this area include Narigama Beach, the Hikkaduwa National Park, the Tsunami Education Center & Museum, Gangarama Maha Vihara, and the Sea Turtle Hatching and Rescue Center. In Hikkaduwa, you'll find yourself sipping a cool beverage on a deck. In this tourist town, corals, unusual fish, and turtles are especially common. Numerous guesthouses, surfing schools, and a peaceful route make this area ideal for lengthy journeys.

Hikkaduwa has been a popular tourist destination since the 1960s, largely because of the immaculate Hikkaduwa Beach. The beach, which is famous for its palm-lined scenery and seductive seas, is ideal for any traveler seeking recreation, leisure, rest, and relaxation. The adrenaline junkie will find plenty of water sports to indulge in, including surfing, diving, and snorkeling. The beach is a great place for the whole family to enjoy a picnic, go swimming, or play some beach volleyball if you want a more laid-back holiday. Hikkaduwa Beach is an expanse of nonstop enjoyment.
